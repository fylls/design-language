Why would PORTFOLIO not be #1 in the hierarchy?

It IS larger and you WANT to read it first, since it’s the first word in the title of the poster.
But remember: the order of reading does not always directly match the visual hierarchy.
The word PORTFOLIO, though it is large in scale, is light in value. The rage of values go from middle gray to white.
This is a lesser or smaller range of value contrast than DAY and the cluster of blobs to the left. 

The same can be said of CALARTS in the gray square.

The values in that unit (CALARTS lettering + square) range from middle gray to white,
which is a smaller range of value compared to both the blob cluster and the 99. 

The word DAY sits inside a little pocket of negative space. The letterforms are solid and dark in value, which creates a high contrast to the white background.
The outline of the letterforms and the countershapes inside them give us reason to linger here.
DAY is not the most important word from a content perspective, but its proximity to PORTFOLIO allows us to put this content together quickly.

To the left of DAY, the cluster of solid bubbles or blobs contain the time and location information for the event.
These solid, dark-value shapes create high contrast to the white background. Because they are clustered together,
they read as a mass or as a single compositional unit. 

Finally, the 99 in a bubble blob to the right of DAY brings our eye over to the right side of the poster.
Despite its small scale, the 99 is relatively high in the hierarchy because of the contrast between its dark value,
solid outline and the large amount of white negative space surrounding it.
