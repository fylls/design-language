In this composition, we see elements aligned to each other
(the green Herb Alpert School of Music aligning to the orange O, M, and A below it) 
or in columns (the information in the right column).]

There is a subtle sense of movement in the white shapes moving through the yellow field,
but it is even and controlled, and compositionally balanced by the white column to the right.


Balance
	Although the poster is divided asymmetrically into a yellow and white block,
	the division is proportional and thus stable because we can see and feel order in the breaking up of space. 

	In other words, if we imagine the poster divided into a 4-column grid, the white column on the right would occupy 1 of the columns,
	and the yellow block would occupy 3 columns. The orange title type is also centered in the poster, adding to the sense of compositional balance. 


Harmony
	The poster is full of lovely detail and contrast, in both the imagery and the typography. But there is nothing extreme or that challenges us.

Evenness
	The poster, overall, does not have any dynamic or exaggerated movement or strong axes that drive the positioning of the elements. 
	But like the other level examples we’ve seen in this video, the quiet harmonious composition gives the viewer space to linger over details.
